Natu Kodi

What Is Natu Kodi Kura?

Natu Kodi Kura is a popular country chicken curry recipe that is part of Andhra cuisine. Natu Kodi,
meaning country chicken is a rich gravy made with different spices. The typical village-style recipe is
cooked on a wood fire in mud pots. All the ingredients are mixed and slow-cooked to perfection for a
delicious, smoky flavour.



Natu Kodi is enjoyed with minapa garelu, also known as medu vada. However, you can also have it with
rice or roti as well. For that authentic taste, it's important to use country chicken and not broiler
chicken. For this recipe, you can choose to cut the chicken either into small pieces or larger-sized
pieces.

INGREDIENTS

    INGREDIENTS
  1. For the spice paste
  2. 2 tbsp poppy seeds
  3. 1 tbsp Coriander Seeds
  4. 2 tsp Guntur chilli powder
  5. tbsp ½grated coconut
  6. 2 tsp turmeric powder
  7. 6 to 7 ginger slices
  8. 6 to 7 garlic cloves
  9. 1 tsp salt
  10. A little bit of water
  11. For the chicken
  12. 7 pieces to 8of country chicken
  13. 1 tsp chilli powder
  14. tsp ½turmeric powder
  15. 3 tbsp of oil
  16. For the base
  17. Few curry leaves
  18. 4 green chillies
  19. 1 finely chopped onion
  20. 1 cups ½of water
  21. 1 tsp garam masala
  22. 3 tbsp to 4oil
  23. Salt - to taste
  24. Coriander leaves to garnish

Instructions

  1. In a pan, add the poppy seeds, coriander seeds, Guntur chilli powder, coconut, garlic, ginger, turmeric powder and salt. Dry roast all the ingredients well.
  2. In a mortar and pestle, add the roasted ingredients and grind with a little water to form a paste. Keep aside.
  3. In a pressure cooker, heat some oil. Add the chicken pieces and sear. Add turmeric and chilli powder.
  4. Add a little water and cook for about four to five whistles.
  5. In a pot or wok, add oil, curry leaves, green chillies, and onions. Sauté until the onions are soft and brown.
  6. Add the cooked chicken and the spice paste. Sauté for about three to four minutes.
  7. Add water, salt and garam masala. Allow the gravy to simmer for a few minutes.
  8. Turn off the heat and garnish with coriander leaves. Serve hot. Enjoy!
